💡 Today's Health Tips

  • Monitor fasting blood glucose every morning before eating.
  • Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise daily.
  • Limit refined carbohydrates and sugary beverages.
  • Stay hydrated — drink 8–10 glasses of water each day.
  • Check feet daily for cuts, blisters, or unusual sores.

📅 Key Health Reminders

  • HbA1c test recommended every 3 months.
  • Annual eye exam for diabetic retinopathy screening.
  • Lipid profile and kidney function tests every 6 months.
  • Keep emergency glucose tabs accessible at all times.
  • Schedule next consultation with your endocrinologist.

🧬 Diabetes Types Overview

  • Type 1: Autoimmune — insulin production ceases completely.
  • Type 2: Lifestyle-linked — insulin resistance develops over time.
  • Gestational: Occurs during pregnancy, requires monitoring.
  • Prediabetes: Blood sugar above normal but below diabetes threshold.

📋 Diabetes Management Guidelines

Evidence-based recommendations from World Health Organization (WHO) for effective diabetes prevention and management.

🩸

Blood Sugar Targets

  • Fasting plasma glucose: 80–130 mg/dL (ADA target)
  • Post-meal (2hr): <180 mg/dL after meals
  • HbA1c goal: <7% for most adults with diabetes
  • Bedtime glucose: 90–150 mg/dL
  • Test fasting glucose daily, post-meal as directed
🥗

Dietary Recommendations

  • Diabetic Plate Method (½ veg, ¼ protein, ¼ carbs)
  • Choose low glycaemic index foods (GI <55)
  • Limit added sugars to <25g per day (WHO)
  • Increase dietary fibre: 25–38g/day
  • Avoid trans fats; prefer unsaturated fats
  • Limit sodium to <2300 mg/day
🏃

Physical Activity

  • 150 min/week of moderate aerobic activity minimum
  • Resistance training at least 2 days per week
  • Break prolonged sitting every 30 minutes
  • Walking 10,000 steps/day lowers glucose significantly
  • Monitor blood glucose before and after exercise
💊

Medication Adherence

  • Never skip prescribed insulin or oral hypoglycaemics
  • Metformin is first-line therapy for Type 2 diabetes
  • Rotate insulin injection sites to prevent lipodystrophy
  • Store insulin between 2–8°C; discard if frozen
  • Report hypoglycaemic episodes (<70 mg/dL) to doctor
🚨

Warning Signs & Emergencies

  • Hypoglycaemia (<70 mg/dL): trembling, confusion, sweating
  • Hyperglycaemia (>250 mg/dL): extreme thirst, frequent urination
  • DKA: fruity breath, nausea, abdominal pain
  • Carry fast-acting carbohydrates (glucose tabs) always
  • Emergency care for glucose <54 or >400 mg/dL
🔍

Routine Monitoring

  • HbA1c: every 3 months (6 months if well-controlled)
  • Eye exam: annually for diabetic retinopathy
  • Foot exam: every visit — sensation, pulses, skin
  • Kidney function (eGFR, urine albumin): annually
  • Lipid profile: at least once per year
